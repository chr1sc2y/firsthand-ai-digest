Executive Summary

The strongest signal is that AI adoption pressure is shifting from "which frontier model?" to "which model, where, and at what marginal cost?"

Facts: Aaron Levie argued that token costs have become a hot enterprise topic and predicted model routing will become a differentiator for applied AI companies with strong workflow-specific evals (source). Aravind Srinivas said Perplexity is working with Intel to bring Personal Computer, local models, and hybrid inference to Intel Core Ultra Series 3 laptops (source); Intel's own developer material frames hybrid AI as local, cloud, or combined execution for security and performance (Intel).

Analysis: The near-term opportunity is orchestration, not just raw model access: routing, local privacy boundaries, eval harnesses, and workflow-aware cost controls are becoming product features.

Implications

Developers

  • Instrument token cost per workflow, not just per model call.
  • Design tasks so routing can choose local, cheap, or frontier execution.
  • Expect AI coding interfaces to expand into voice and mobile contexts.

Startups

  • Domain evals and routing policies are defensible product assets.
  • Hybrid local/cloud is a wedge for privacy-sensitive verticals.
  • Do not sell agentic features without measurable cost and quality controls.

Researchers

  • Adaptability remains a separate axis from knowledge scale.
  • Game agents need evaluation beyond reward maximization: narrative quality, control, safety, and player trust.
  • Macro-AI claims need stronger empirical grounding before operational decisions.

Enterprise Buyers

  • Ask vendors for routing logic, eval evidence, and cost attribution.
  • Test local inference claims against real privacy, latency, and admin constraints.
  • Budget for orchestration and governance, not just model subscriptions.

Trend Judgment

The applied AI layer is starting to look like a control plane: it chooses models, venues, cost bands, and privacy boundaries. Winners will likely combine evals, routing, and user-context integration rather than relying on one model provider.
