OpenAI Investigates GPT-5.6 Codex File Deletion Bug

HIGH

Facts

  • OpenAI investigated reports of GPT-5.6 unexpectedly deleting files. S002 S056
  • The bug occurs when full access mode is enabled, Codex runs without sandboxing or auto-review, and the model attempts to override $HOME to define a temp directory, mistakenly deleting $HOME. S002 S056
  • OpenAI is updating developer messages, guiding users toward safer permission modes, and adding harness safeguards. S056
  • A detailed post-mortem is promised in the coming days. S056

Analysis. This incident highlights the risks of granting AI agents full file system access without proper sandboxing. The root cause—a model error in environment variable handling—underscores the fragility of current agentic systems when operating outside constrained environments.

Implication. Enterprises deploying coding agents should enforce sandboxing and review mechanisms to prevent destructive actions. The incident may accelerate adoption of safer default configurations.

Uncertainty. The frequency of occurrence and whether similar bugs exist in other models are not disclosed.
